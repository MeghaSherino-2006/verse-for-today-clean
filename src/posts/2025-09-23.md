### Verse for Today

> ‘What if some were unfaithful? Will their unfaithfulness nullify God’s faithfulness? Not at all! Let God be true, and every human being a liar. As it is written: “So that you may be proved right when you speak and prevail when you judge.” (Romans 3:3-4)

---

### Reflection

It is God (ultimately) before whom we are to ‘account’ for and from this accountability springs every annotation of faithfulness. King David, whom God delighted in, was aware of this Divine principle. He openly admitted that he was wrong and God was right in all His ways and judgments, because he knew that retribution and atonement for sins came from God alone. In the context of God’s people of the Old Testament – Israel, God proved Himself trustworthy in all His promises, though the people sinned and were unfaithful on their part. By not holding back His son Jesus, whom God sent for us, to redeem humanity, God proved His mercy, love, and above all His truthfulness. Therefore, all ‘the Created’ bow down before the majesty of Jesus, who has authority over all and whose word is the final judgment. To whom He imputes no transgression, that one is blessed. You and I are called to walk with Jesus, to be faithful to His Word, and do His will. If the Son of Man has set you free, You are indeed free. Amen
