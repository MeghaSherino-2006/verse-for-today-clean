### Verse for Today

> 'Let us then go to Him outside the camp and bear the abuse He endured' (Hebrews 13:13)

---

### Reflection

The camp is the flesh, 'our body' that we nourish and comfort daily. How we wish and pray for 'a pleasant weather', for better resources, favourable situations and people in our lives - how relieved to be with our dear and near, of being appreciated by the crowds, of enjoying success through life's journey, who would not desire for these? By giving us the gift of life, our Creator who loved us, intended all creation for our betterment, but when sin began to dominate human lives, we became enslaved to the Created and abandoned our authority over all to the evil one who claimed to be The Prince of this world. When Jesus, the Son of God incarnated on earth, He underwent tremendous humiliation, endured' pain in His sinless body and submitted to be crucified, to purge from us the curse of sin - the slavery of mankind. Do we understand and appreciate the price of our Salvation? how we regained our authority in Jesus. Therefore let not the flesh prevail, but let us be led by the promptings of the Holy Spirit. Let us move out of the camp (our comfort zone) to accept life's sufferings for saving souls, in thankfulness for what we received from Jesus. Amen
